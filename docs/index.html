Residual interpretation

The residual component does not look like pure white noise. The residual series still shows strong short-run dependence, with lag-1 autocorrelation of 0.93, and the Ljung-Box test rejects a pure-noise process at both 13 and 26 lags (p-values 0.0000 and 0.0000).

The largest leftover disturbances cluster around winter transition weeks rather than appearing evenly spread through the year. Absolute residuals are largest in January, and the most volatile weeks are 3, 2, 4, 16. In the figure, this leftover component is split heuristically into a smoother Structured Residual line, meant to capture winter weather shocks and storage-regime shifts, and a faster-moving Noise line for the remaining short-run variation.
